What Rapid Cortex Does
Platform overview and design philosophy
ℹ️ Rapid Cortex is decision support — not a replacement for your CAD, telephony, or radio systems. AI suggestions are advisory; your agency's SOPs, medical control, and supervisor direction always take precedence.
📋 Incident Management
Open incidents for your agency appear in the queue. Selecting one launches the live workspace with transcript and AI recommendations.
🤖 AI-Assisted Analysis
The AI panel provides suggestions, protocol hints, and analysis within schema constraints. Outputs are bounded and auditable.
📡 Live Transcripts
Segments arrive from simulation, demo scenarios, or integrated external feeds. Treat transcript text as sensitive operational data.
🔐
Signing In
Authentication and access
1
Open your organization's URL
Navigate to your jurisdiction's address: www.rapidcortex.us/[city-or-county]. Some organizations have separate training and production URLs — confirm you're on the correct one.
2
Enter your credentials
Use the email and password issued by your administrator. Authentication is powered by Amazon Cognito. If you've never logged in before, you may be prompted to set a new password.
3
Your role determines your view
Roles are assigned by your agency administrator. If your role or agency appears incorrect after signing in, contact your agency admin or platform operator.
⚠️ Can't sign in? Contact your agency IT or Rapid Cortex support per your contract. Do not share login credentials with colleagues.

Roles & Permissions
What each role can access

Roles are stored in Cognito as custom:role claims and enforced server-side on every request. The UI mirrors RBAC for experience only — the backend is authoritative.

Role Access Typical Use
dispatcher Live workspace: incident queue, transcript, AI panel, protocol hints 911 call-takers and dispatch staff on active shifts
supervisor All dispatcher access + flagged incident review queue Shift supervisors validating AI output and escalations
admin User management, agency settings, audit logs (own agency only) Agency IT leads and communications center managers
platform_superadmin Cross-agency operations: create/suspend agencies, list all agencies, invite to any agency Rapid Cortex internal platform operators only

Dispatcher Workspace
The live operations view at /dashboard
Incident List
Open incidents for your agency appear here when the API is configured. Selecting an incident opens the full live workspace. If no incidents appear, see the Runbook.
Transcript Panel
Segments arrive from simulation, demo scenarios, or (when integrated) external feeds. Treat all transcript content as sensitive operational data subject to your agency's data policy.
AI Panel
Suggestions are decision support, not orders. Always follow your agency's SOPs, medical control direction, and supervisor guidance — the AI augments, never overrides.
Protocol Guidance
When enabled, the protocol engine surfaces approved phrases and next-step guidance. These are derived from versioned protocol packs aligned to your agency's configuration.
✓ The protocol engine and LLM operate independently. The engine selects steps and approved phrases; the LLM classifies and narrates within a bounded schema. Facts cannot be added in post-processing.
📂
History & After-Action Review
Completed incident archive at /history

Browse completed or archived incidents for review and after-action discussion. Filters help narrow the list by date, type, or status. Availability of rows depends on API connectivity and your agency's data-retention configuration.

History access follows your role — dispatchers can view their own incidents; supervisors can view agency-wide history; admins have full read access within their agency.

🔍
Supervisor Review
Flagged incident inspection at /review

Supervisors use the Review area to inspect incidents that require a second look — for example, AI-flagged escalations, protocol deviations, or calls queued by dispatchers for supervisor input.

The platform provides queues and views. Workflow specifics — who must review what and within what timeframe — follow your agency's standard operating procedures.

ℹ️ Rapid Cortex surfaces what needs review; your SOP defines the policy. The software does not automate disposition decisions.
⚙️
Admin Hub
Agency management at /admin
Users
List and manage Cognito-backed users within your agency: create accounts, update role and agency attributes, deactivate departing staff. Agency admins are scoped to their own agency only.
Audit Log
Read agency-scoped audit events when enabled. Audit covers security- and workflow-sensitive actions including user provisioning, invite lifecycle, and cross-tenant access attempts.
Settings & Integrations
Integration status panel shows active connectors. Many connectors are planned until your organization completes vendor integration work. See CAD & Motorola.
Billing
Billing screens are environment-dependent (Square-oriented in pilot deployments). Treat financial actions as production-sensitive and follow your finance team's approval process.

Inviting a New User

1
Navigate to Admin → Users
Only agency admins and platform superadmins can send invites. Admins are limited to their own agency.
2
Create invite
Enter the invitee's email, select their role (dispatcher, supervisor, or admin), and optionally set an expiry window. The invite is logged in the audit table.
3
Credentials delivered
In pilot deployments, credentials are shared out-of-band. On first login, Cognito prompts a required password change. The user's account then carries the correct agencyId and role claims.

Demo & Training Mode
Scripted scenarios at /demo
🚨 The demo banner is always visible when you are not on live data. Never use demo mode for real operational decisions.

The demo experience is for training and sales walkthroughs using scripted scenarios — for example cardiac arrest or structure fire. Use Start, Pause/Resume, and Reset controls as directed by your trainer. All data paths are identical to production; only the data source differs.

Deploy Steps

1
Clone & install
git clone <repo-url> && npm install
2
Configure environment
Copy .env.example to apps/web/.env.local and set NEXT_PUBLIC_API_BASE (direct mode) or NEXT_PUBLIC_AUTH_PROXY=1 + API_UPSTREAM_BASE (BFF proxy mode). Align Cognito variables with stack outputs.
3
Build
npm run build — builds workspaces in dependency order: shared → protocols → integrations → security → api → web.
4
Deploy API
./scripts/deploy.sh dev (or staging / prod). Optional env vars for domain wiring: API_DOMAIN_CERT_ARN, ROUTE53_HOSTED_ZONE_ID, CNAME targets.
5
Smoke test
./scripts/post-deploy-smoke.sh dev us-east-1 — validates health endpoint, auth gate, and custom domain if configured.

Key Environment Variables

VariablePurpose
NEXT_PUBLIC_API_BASEFull API URL when browser calls the API directly with bearer tokens
NEXT_PUBLIC_AUTH_PROXYSet to 1 to use the Next.js BFF proxy (cookies instead of exposed tokens)
API_UPSTREAM_BASEServer-only: SAM API base URL when using auth proxy
COGNITO_USER_POOL_IDMust match the pool used by the API JWT authorizer
INTEGRATION_TRANSCRIPT_CONNECTOR_MODEoff | shadow | on — controls transcript connector rollout
PRIMARY_PROVIDERAI provider selection; set alongside API keys

First-Time Agency Onboarding

  • Create or select a Cognito User Pool for the target environment
  • Deploy the SAM stack for the target stage (dev / staging / prod)
  • Create the agency row via the admin API or seed script
  • Invite the initial agency admin with correct agencyId and admin role
  • Agency admin invites dispatchers and supervisors via the Admin Hub
  • Smoke-test: health endpoint, sign-in, incident list, audit log

Operations Runbook
For on-call engineers and DevOps responding to incidents

Health Checks

CheckEndpointExpected
API liveness GET /api/health 200 — JSON with "status":"ok"
Auth gate GET /api/me 401 without Authorization header
Custom domain Same paths on ApiCustomDomainUrl Same as above when configured

Common Issues

SYM 1 "API not configured" or empty incident list
  • Check: browser network calls return 401 / 403 / 502
  • Verify: NEXT_PUBLIC_API_BASE or proxy + API_UPSTREAM_BASE match stack outputs
  • Verify: user JWT contains custom:agencyId and custom:role
SYM 2 All API requests return 401
  • Check: Authorization: Bearer <id token> present (direct mode) or cookie flow (proxy mode)
  • Verify: Cognito app client ID and issuer match the pool used by the API authorizer
SYM 3 Elevated 5xx from API Gateway
  • Check: CloudWatch Logs for the Lambda behind the failing route
  • Check: DynamoDB throttling; IAM AccessDenied on table or S3
  • Mitigate: roll back recent template change; throttle on HttpApi DefaultRouteSettings already set
SYM 4 Integration status always "off" / no external transcript
Expected unless INTEGRATION_TRANSCRIPT_CONNECTOR_MODE is shadow or on and adapters are implemented. See CAD & Motorola Integration.

CAD & Motorola Integration
How Rapid Cortex connects to dispatch systems
⚠️ The live CAD connector is not turnkey in this release. The admin UI shows CAD as planned. Integration requires a scoped engagement with your vendor and Rapid Cortex engineering.

"Motorola CAD" is not one universal API. Integration depends on your specific product generation, contract scope, hosting topology, and CJIS networking requirements. Treat it as a discovery and design exercise.

Phase A Discovery Weeks — involve vendor TAM/PS
  • Inventory systems: CAD product name/version, RMS, logging recorder, 911 CPE, any middleware
  • List integration options the vendor supports for your deployment (APIs, message buses, XML/JSON feeds, webhooks)
  • Define data scope: which fields Rapid Cortex needs and what must never leave CAD
  • Agree on security path: CJIS alignment, TLS 1.2+, authentication method, audit requirements
Phase B Read-only Shadow Mode First technical milestone
Ingest incident updates or transcript-adjacent events into Rapid Cortex without controlling dispatch. Use INTEGRATION_TRANSCRIPT_CONNECTOR_MODE=shadow to compare external feed behavior to mock/live paths before full reliance.
Phase C Live — Operator Enabled After validation
Enable on for allow-listed agencies after shadow validation. Maintain human-in-the-loop for any suggestion that could influence dispatch or medical decisions.
Phase D Write-back Requires explicit governance
Automatic CAD updates are a non-goal for MVP. Any write-back requires dual authorization, idempotency, replay protection, and audit events. Reopening requires legal and vendor review.

Motorola Engagement Checklist

  • Confirm product names, versions, and hosting model with your Motorola Solutions account team
  • Request the integration catalog for your SKU: protocols, rate limits, test environments
  • Request a non-production endpoint for Rapid Cortex development and testing
  • Agree on network path (public internet vs private connectivity) and IP allow-lists
  • Agree on data elements available in each message and retention constraints
  • Plan fallback: if the feed stalls, dispatch continues in CAD per your SOP

Multi-Tenant & RBAC
How agencies are isolated and authorized
ℹ️ This specification is CJIS-aligned by design — but alignment is not a certification claim. Follow your agency's CJIS policy for compliance obligations.
Tenant = Agency
Every customer organization is an Agency (tenant). All operational and configuration data is scoped by agencyId. No email-domain tenancy — domain may be a UX hint only, never a security boundary.
JWT Claims
Cognito ID tokens supply sub, email, custom:agencyId, and custom:role. The backend is authoritative for authorization on every request.
Platform Superadmin
Uses a sentinel tenant ID (__platform__) and can bypass read tenant boundaries only on explicitly allow-listed operations. Cannot dispatch on behalf of agencies.
Audit Trail
Sensitive actions write to the Audit table with actor (sub), target agency, event type, and timestamp. Event types include agency lifecycle, invite lifecycle, and user admin actions.

Agency Onboarding Flow (Platform Superadmin)

1
Create agency
POST /api/agencies — creates agency row + default config. Emits agency.created audit event.
2
Invite initial admin
POST /api/agencies/:id/invites with role admin. Invite enters pending state; emits invite.created.
3
First login
Invitee receives credentials out-of-band (pilot) or via future email link. Cognito FORCE_CHANGE_PASSWORD completes on first login; claims carry agencyId and role.
4
Agency admin provisions staff
Agency admin invites dispatchers and supervisors via Admin Hub. All invite activity is audited.

Architecture Overview
Monorepo structure and AWS-native principles
apps/web
Next.js UI
Browser app, BFF routes, Cognito session bridging. Deployable to Vercel, ECS, Amplify, or any container host.
Next.js · React
apps/api
Serverless API
AWS Lambda + API Gateway handlers, services, and repositories. JWT authorizer (Cognito) on all routes except documented public paths.
Lambda · API Gateway · SAM
packages/shared
Shared Types
TypeScript types and Zod schemas shared across API and web to prevent UI/API drift.
TypeScript · Zod
packages/security
RBAC & Audit
RBAC helpers, audit/redaction utilities, policy types, and the audit event vocabulary used by all handlers.
Security · Policy
packages/integrations
Vendor Adapters
Vendor-agnostic adapter interfaces for audio, CAD, and event feeds. Includes mock implementations for development and testing.
CadAdapter · MockCadAdapter
infra/
Infrastructure
SAM template defining all AWS resources: API Gateway, Lambda, DynamoDB, S3, Cognito, CloudWatch, Route53. Env matrices and GovCloud migration notes.
